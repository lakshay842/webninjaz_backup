Soot Removal

It refers to the process of cleaning and eliminating soot deposits from various surfaces. Soot is a black powdery or flaky substance composed primarily of carbon, produced by incomplete combustion of organic matter.

It is commonly found in the exhaust emissions of vehicles, industrial processes, and residential heating systems. Soot accumulates on walls, ceilings, chimneys, vents, and household items, causing stains and potential health hazards.

Corrosion Removal

Corrosion removal is the process of eliminating or mitigating the effects of corrosion on various materials, particularly metals. Corrosion is the gradual degradation of materials, often due to chemical reactions with their environment.

This process weakens the material, undermines its structural stability, and shortens its lifespan. Therefore, the goal of corrosion removal is to halt or reverse this degradation, preserving the integrity and durability of the affected materials.

Why laser cleaning?
